Real world applications of Virtual Reality

While Virtual Reality in it's current state is still under development, it's already being implemented and applicated in our world today.

Virtual Reality & Gaming

VR gaming is the application of a 3D artificial environment to computer games. The environments are created with VR software and presented to the users in a way that supersede the real-world environment, creating suspension of disbelief and helping the user experience the VR environment as real. VR gaming might involve a 3D image that can be explored interactively on a computing device by manipulating keys, mouse or touchscreen. More sophisticated and immersive examples include VR headsets, wrap around display screens and VR rooms augmented with wearable computers and sensory components, such as scents and haptics devices for tactile feedback.

Virtual Reality & Movies

IMAX screens offer immersive viewing experiences that rival those seen in theme parks. D-Box seats offer movie viewing in 4D, allowing you to feel on screen explosions and ride along in a car chase as your seat tilts with wild turns. Then there’s the behind the scenes technology that helps producers and directors make movies: tech like motion-capture and computer-generated imagery has been used by filmmakers for a long time, but no technology has both worked as a draw for audiences and helped Hollywood make movies. VR is being used in the film industry to tell stories and immerse movie goers in brand new adventure.

Virtual Reality & Education

Education is the base for a thriving society, and the transfer of knowledge has been a top priority for civilizations since the very beginning. People are constantly looking for ways to make knowledge transfer more easily, quickly and more effectively. In the era of digital devices, we have the opportunity to enable better learning with technology. VR seems to be the natural next step for the evolution of education. VR education can transform the way educational content is delivered. VR can be used in classrooms to enhance student learning and engagement. It works on the premise of creating a virtual world, real or imagined, and allows users to interact with it. Being immersed in what you’re learning motivates you to fully understand it. It’ll require less cognitive load to process the information.

Virtual Reality & Medicine

Virtual reality is used in many areas of healthcare which range from diagnosis, treatment, e.g. surgery, rehab and counselling. It is also used to train the next generation of doctors, paramedics and other medical personnel and has shown a range of benefits from doing so. There are several advantages of virtual reality in healthcare related to medical/ surgical training, preventative medicine, counselling and architectural design of new hospitals. For example, medical students can perform “hands on” procedures but in a safe and controlled setting. They are able to make mistakes and learn from them but in an environment where there is no risk to the patient. They interact with a virtual patient and as a result of this learn skills which they can apply in the real world.

General inqueries

What is Virtual Reality?

Virtual reality is the immersion into a computer-generated environment with the use of headsets, sensory gloves and special treadmills.

What is the difference between Virtual Reality and Augmented reality?

Augmented reality is when an overlay is placed on a real-world scenario, while virtual reality is complete immersion into another world.

Where can I play Virtual Reality video games?

You can purchase video games and equipment and play from the comfort of your own home. Alternatively, there are also some virtual reality arcades available.

Where can I purchase Virtual Reality headsets?

Most Electronic stores sell virtual reality headsets.
